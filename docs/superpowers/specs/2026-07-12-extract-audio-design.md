# Design: `extract-audio` — prove every sound we ship against the ROM

**Date:** 2026-07-12
**Status:** Approved (brainstorm)
**Author:** Architect (The History Men)

## Problem

Every game in the fleet makes noise. **Almost none of it is provably authentic**,
and we currently have no way to find out which parts are.

What we ship today, and what it actually rests on:

| Game | How it sounds | What it rests on |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| tempest | `.wav` samples (R2) | 9 of 13 baked from a ROM; the rest are community rips |
| star-wars | `.wav` samples (R2): sfx + music + speech | hand-transcribed from a **third-party disassembly** |
| asteroids | `.wav` samples (R2) | community field recordings — no ROM anywhere in the chain |
| battlezone | runtime synthesis | **entirely invented** — `src/shell/audio.ts` asserts "no ROM register data exists to bake" |
| red-baron | runtime synthesis | one ROM-exact tone (`TK`); `TP/BN/WP/TH` "synthesised to their documented shape" |
| lobby | silent | — |

Three structural failures sit behind that table.

**1. The provenance chains are broken, and one is severed outright.**
`star-wars/tools/speech-bake/speech-data.mjs` — 577 lines, 23 raw LPC hex blobs,
the most opaque artifact in the entire fleet — declares in its own header:

> *"GENERATED by tools/speech-bake (gen-speech-data.mjs) from the gitignored
> reference disassembly — do not hand-edit."*

**`gen-speech-data.mjs` does not exist in the repo.** The generator is gone and its
input was gitignored. That data is now unreproducible and unauditable: a wall of
hex that no human can eyeball and no script can regenerate. We are shipping it.

**2. The ground truth was unavailable — until today.** Every transcription above was
made from disassemblies and ROM images because the *original Atari sound source*
wasn't on disk. `just vendor-source` (2026-07-12) changed that. We now hold, for
the first time:

```
tempest-source-text/ALSOUN.MAC          battlezone-source-text/BZSOUN.MAC
red-baron-source-text/RBSOUN.MAC        star-wars-1983-source-text/SNDPM.MAC
star-wars-1983-source-text/SWMUS.{MAC,SND}   star-wars-1983-source-text/SWVOC{2,3}.MAC
```

plus the `.MAP` symbol tables and the ROM chip dumps themselves. `BZSOUN.MAC` has
sat unread while battlezone's audio was invented from scratch.

**3. Transcription error is not hypothetical.** The Asteroids shot-muzzle
mistranscription (a `ror` signed-halve read as a 1.5× multiply) deflected every
shot by ~11° and survived until someone re-derived it from the ROM. A hand-copied
table is a claim, not a fact, and we currently have no instrument that can tell
the difference.

## Goal

A command that, for any game, walks an evidence chain from the original Atari
source to a rendered `.wav` and **refuses to skip a link** — then reports, per
sound, whether what we ship is what the machine actually made.

The deliverable is **certainty**, expressed as a checked-in verdict table. The
`.wav` files are evidence backing the verdicts, not the product.

**Non-goals:**

- **Not an asset pipeline.** It does not upload to R2. Promoting a ROM-verified
  render into `arcade-assets` is a separate, deliberate act.
- **Not a catalog.** It does not exist to accumulate sounds. A sound it cannot
  prove is a *finding*, not a gap to be filled.
- **Not an audio-architecture change.** Whether battlezone and red-baron abandon
  runtime synthesis for ROM-verified samples through `@arcade/shared/audio` is a
  decision this tool *informs* and a later story *makes*.

## The hard rule: no fallbacks

**A sound that cannot complete the chain is reported `UNVERIFIED`. It is never
quietly satisfied from the hand-transcribed table, the community rip, or the
`.wav` we already ship.**

This is the single most important property of the design and the easiest to
erode. Every fallback is a lie told to a future reader: it converts "we could not
prove this" into "this looks fine," which is precisely the state we are in now and
precisely what the tool exists to escape. If the tool can be made to emit a full
green table by leaning on existing artifacts, it is worthless.

Concretely: the per-game adapters **may not import** `tempest/tools/pokey-bake/sfx-data.mjs`,
`star-wars/tools/pokey-bake/sfx-data.mjs`, `star-wars/tools/speech-bake/speech-data.mjs`,
or `red-baron/src/shell/pokey.ts`. Those are **comparison targets**, read only to
be judged.

## Design

### Interface

```
just extract-audio <game> [--render] [--out DIR]   # audit one game
just extract-audio-all                              # the whole fleet
# → scripts/extract-audio.mjs <game> [--render] [--out <dir>]
# → scripts/extract-audio.mjs --all
```

- `<game>` — `tempest` | `star-wars` | `battlezone` | `red-baron` | `asteroids`.
- `--render` — additionally decode each verified sound to `.wav` under `--out`
  (default `out/audio/<game>/`, gitignored). Without it the run is a pure audit:
  parse, locate, compare, report. Rendering is for **ears**; the verdict does not
  depend on it.
- Exit code is **non-zero if any sound is `MISMATCH` or `UNVERIFIED`**, so this
  can gate CI once the fleet is green.

Reads the vendored trees recorded in `docs/reference-sources.md` — the pristine
clone for ROM bytes, the greppable copy for source text.

### The audit chain

Each sound is a row; each row must survive five links.

1. **PARSE** — read the game's sound source (`ALSOUN.MAC`, `BZSOUN.MAC`,
   `RBSOUN.MAC`, `SNDPM.MAC`, `SWVOC{2,3}.MAC`) and recover its data tables,
   carrying the original labels and comments through as provenance. The Atari
   sources are `.RADIX 16` MACRO-11/6809 assembler; the tables are `.BYTE`/`.WORD`
   directives.
2. **LOCATE** — resolve each table's address from the game's `.MAP` symbol table.
   These are real and precise — `red-baron-source-text/RBARON.MAP` yields the
   `RBSOUN` module base and length (`RBSOUN 71C4 0104`) plus symbols
   (`SNDON 7259`, `MODSND 7278`, `CRSHSN 1808`).
3. **VERIFY** — read the ROM at that address and require the parsed bytes to be
   **byte-identical**. This is the load-bearing link: it is what separates a fact
   from a transcription.
4. **RENDER** — decode the verified bytes to PCM through the appropriate engine.
5. **COMPARE** — judge what the game currently ships against the verified truth.

### Verdicts

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| `ROM-VERIFIED` | Parsed → located → byte-equal to ROM → rendered. What we ship matches. |
| `MISMATCH` | The chain completed, and **what we ship is not it**. Includes the ROM truth and the diff. |
| `UNVERIFIED` | The chain broke. Names **the exact link that broke** and why. |
| `NO ROM AUDIO` | No sound ROM exists for this sound, by design of the original hardware. Terminal and honest — not a to-do. |

`NO ROM AUDIO` is asteroids' whole column: the 1979 cabinet has **no sound ROM**,
only a discrete analog board (555 timers, op-amps). Community rips are the
permanent ceiling, and the table should say so out loud instead of leaving a
suggestive blank.

### Architecture

```
scripts/extract-audio.mjs        # CLI + audit driver (thin; mirrors vendor-source.mjs)
scripts/audio/
  games/
    tempest.mjs                  # adapter: source file · MAP · ROM · table format · sound list
    star-wars.mjs
    battlezone.mjs
    red-baron.mjs
    asteroids.mjs                # declares NO ROM AUDIO, with the reason
  parse/
    mac.mjs                      # MACRO-11 .BYTE/.WORD/.RADIX table reader
    map.mjs                      # .MAP symbol table → { symbol: address }
    rom.mjs                      # raw chip dumps + DEC .LDA absolute-loader unpacker
  render/
    pokey.mjs                    # (register, value, time) writes → PCM
    tms5220.mjs                  # LPC bitstream → 8 kHz PCM
    musicvm.mjs                  # tune bytecode → timed POKEY register writes
  wav.mjs                        # PCM → .wav
  vendor/
    pokey.js                     # web-pokey (MIT, 0c6327b) — ONE copy
docs/audio-provenance.md         # the verdict table (checked in)
tests/extract-audio.test.mjs
```

Node, zero dependencies, ES modules — same constraints as `vendor-source.mjs` and
`deploy-r2.mjs`. Pure core exported for unit test; `main()` guarded so tests can
import.

### Three artifact classes

A game's audio is not one kind of thing, and the chain runs over all three:

| Class | Data | Engine |
|-------|------|--------|
| **SFX** | POKEY envelope tables (`ALSOUN`/`BZSOUN`/`RBSOUN`/star-wars FX) | `pokey.mjs` |
| **Music** | Tune bytecode (`SWMUS.MAC` + `SWMUS.SND`) | `musicvm.mjs` → `pokey.mjs` |
| **Speech** | TMS5220 LPC bitstreams (`SWVOC2/3.MAC`) | `tms5220.mjs` |

Star Wars is the only cabinet with all three. Music and speech are star-wars-only;
every game has SFX (except asteroids, which has no sound ROM at all).

### Two of the three engines already exist

The POKEY and TMS5220 renderers are **rescued from duplication**, not written.

- **POKEY** — `web-pokey` (Mariusz Kryński, MIT, commit `0c6327b`) is vendored
  **twice, identically**, at `tempest/tools/pokey-bake/vendor/pokey.js` and
  `star-wars/tools/pokey-bake/vendor/pokey.js`, driven headlessly through a
  `node:vm` shim. One copy moves to `scripts/audio/vendor/pokey.js`.
- **TMS5220** — `star-wars/tools/speech-bake/tms5220.mjs` (182 lines) is an
  independent implementation from TI patent 4,209,804 and the datasheet, with the
  chirp table checksummed (`0x3DA`) against the published decap. It is **not** a
  port of MAME's, which matters: MAME is GPL-2.0 and must not be vendored here.
  It moves to `scripts/audio/render/tms5220.mjs` unchanged.

The third, `musicvm.mjs`, is genuinely new — but it is an **interpreter, not a
synthesizer**. It emits POKEY register writes into the same core. No new DSP.

What was missing was never the synthesis. It was the ground truth — and
`vendor-source` just delivered it.

### Star Wars music: a bytecode VM with its own oracle

The score is not a flat sequence. `SWMUS.MAC` ("STAR WARS TUNES") defines named
instrument envelopes, a 50-entry tune table, and a **little bytecode language with
a call stack**:

```
;	FREQUENCY ENVELOPES          ;	AMPLITUDE ENVELOPES
HRN =2 ;HORN                     SDR =1 ;STEEL DRUMS
TRB =3 ;TROMBONE                 HRD =2 ;HARD EMPHASIS
BAS =4 ;BASSOON                  QKR =3 ;QUICK RISING ATTACK
GLK =5 ;GLOCKINSPIEL(?)          TIE =4 ;STEEL DRUM TIES
WW  =6 ;WOODWINDS

TUNTAB::  .WORD SWNUL, SF2V1..SF2V6, BENV1-4, CNTV1-4, ENDV1-4,
                REBV1-4, RR1-4, TH5V1-4, SW4V1-4, THB1-4, DESV1-4, DARV1-4, TSTV1-4
```

Tunes are grouped as **4 voices each** (the opening `SF2` fanfare gets 6), across
the sound board's quad-POKEY. The named tunes include the Star Wars theme, Ben's
theme, the Rebel themes and Darth's.

**Opcode set** (high bit set = command, low = note, `00 00` ends a tune):

```
.NOTE  .CKEY  .CRATE  .NKEY  .NVOL  .NRATE  .SYN  .VC  .AENV     ; voice + note
.CALL  .GOSUB  .RETURN  .LOOP / .ENDL  .ENDT                     ; control flow
```

`.CALL n` invokes another tune from `TUNTAB` and `.GOSUB` calls a phrase by
address, so tunes reuse each other — the VM needs a real call stack and loop
counters, not a cursor.

**Music carries an oracle no other class has.** The two files are the *same music
in two independent encodings*: `SWMUS.SND` is the composer's symbolic source, and
`SWMUS.MAC` is the assembled bytes with the mnemonics preserved as comments beside
them.

```
SWMUS.SND               SWMUS.MAC
SF2V1: .CALL 6          SF2V1: ;.CALL 6
       .CKEY 0                 .BYTE 8D, 6
       .CALL 5                 ;.CKEY 0
       .ENDT                   .BYTE 85, 0
```

Parsing both and requiring them to agree checks the byte encoding against the
composer's intent — an independent verification of link 1 that SFX and speech
cannot get. It also means the opcode *names* are recoverable from the data itself,
so the VM is documented by its own source.

**The substantive work — and the main risk — is the driver, not the data.** The
opcodes are legible; their *semantics* (how `.NRATE`/`.CRATE` map to tempo, how a
named envelope becomes AUDF/AUDC writes, how `.SYN`/`.VC` gate a voice) live in the
sound-board code (`SNDPM.MAC`, `SNDAUD.MAC`), and porting that interpreter is the
real job. If a semantic cannot be established from the source, the affected tune is
`UNVERIFIED` — it is **not** approximated to something that sounds close. A tune
that merely sounds right is exactly the failure this tool exists to detect.

### Per-game adapters are deliberate

The table formats genuinely differ, and a universal parser would be a fiction:

| Game | Format |
|------|--------|
| tempest | 6-byte ALSOUN envelope `[value, beats, delta, count, restart, stop]`, one record per POKEY register, ticked at the ~250 Hz sound IRQ |
| star-wars | 4-byte FX record `[count, duration, value, delta]`, quad-POKEY (8 channels) |
| red-baron | 8-byte register-offset table (one slot per AUDF/AUDC, `null` = untouched) + 4-byte `STVAL·FRCNT·CHANGE·NUMBER` steps at 4 ms |
| battlezone | **unknown — `BZSOUN.MAC` has never been read.** Establishing it is the adapter's job. |
| asteroids | none (analog) |

Each adapter declares its source file, `.MAP`, ROM, format, and sound list. The
shared machinery is the chain, the ROM check, the engines, and the report.

### Star Wars speech: the oracle is a gift

`SWVOC2.MAC` is not a disassembly. Its own header reads *"ROM/PROM data file
created by UPLOAD V1.0, 22-JAN-1983"* — **it is the speech ROM**, dumped by Atari
as `.BYTE` directives. And it opens with an address table:

```
SPKVTB::
;ADDRESS LOOKUP TABLE - START ADDRESS, STOP ADDRESS
	.WORD VOCAB+0038,VOCAB+0171	;USE THE FORCE, LUKE
	.WORD VOCAB+0172,VOCAB+02E7	;THE FORCE WILL BE WITH YOU, ALWAYS
	.WORD VOCAB+02E8,VOCAB+038C	;REMEMBER (used with previous phrase, with pause between)
```

Exact byte ranges for all 23 lines, phrase-labelled by the people who recorded
them in 1983. Speech therefore needs **no address hunting and no `.LDA`
unpacking**: parse the `.BYTE` rows into a `VOCAB` blob, slice by `SPKVTB`, decode
with `tms5220.mjs`. Link 2 and link 3 collapse into the source itself, which makes
speech both the highest-value and the *cheapest* target.

**Open question the audit will settle:** our `speech-data.mjs` has
`n:2 = "remember"`, but `SPKVTB` entry 2 is *"the force will be with you, always"* —
`remember` is entry **3**. Either the disassembly's `SpchTab` indexes differently
or a line is mis-mapped. This is a byte-for-byte question and the tool answers it
definitively; no one should guess in the meantime.

### Guards / edge cases

- **Vendored source missing** → error naming the exact `just vendor-source` command
  to run. Never a partial audit against a half-present tree.
- **`.LDA` unpacking** (tempest `TEMPST.LDA`, star-wars `SNDAUX.LDA`) is the DEC
  absolute-loader format: framed blocks of `[0x01 0x00, count, load address, data,
  checksum]`. Blocks are placed at their load addresses to reconstruct the image.
  A checksum failure is a hard error, not a warning. Raw chip dumps (asteroids
  `A35131.1A–1E`, battlezone `0364xx.01`, red-baron `0369xx/0370xx.01`) need no
  unpacking.
- **Adapter cannot find a table** → that sound is `UNVERIFIED` with the failing
  link named. The run continues; other sounds are still judged.
- **`--all`**: one game's failure does not abort the rest. The summary lists every
  game, and the exit code reflects the worst verdict in the fleet.
- **Byte-equality is exact.** No tolerance, no fuzzy match, no "close enough."
  Fuzziness here is indistinguishable from the bug we are hunting.

### Fidelity oracle

The tool must **reproduce tempest's known-good bake byte-for-byte**. Tempest's
nine ★ authentic sounds were derived from ROM `136002-136.lm1 @ $cc2d` and the
verified address formula `addr = $cbcf + 2 × v` (documented in
`tempest/docs/ux/2026-06-28-pokey-sfx-rom-map.md`). Rendering those nine through
the new chain — parsed from `ALSOUN.MAC`, located via `.MAP`, ROM-verified — must
produce PCM identical to the old chain's.

The reference PCM is **regenerated, not assumed**: `tempest/tools/pokey-bake/out/`
is gitignored and absent from a fresh checkout, so the oracle runs the committed
`tempest/tools/pokey-bake/bake-sfx.mjs` to produce it, then diffs. (Generating the
comparison target from `sfx-data.mjs` does not breach the no-fallback rule — the
hand table is being *judged*, and the oracle is the judgement. It is never an input
to a verdict.)

That is the proof the new path is sound *before* we trust its verdict on anything
else. If the oracle passes, a `MISMATCH` elsewhere is a real finding; if it fails,
the tool is broken and every other verdict is noise.

## Files

- `scripts/extract-audio.mjs` — **new**.
- `scripts/audio/**` — **new** (adapters, parsers, engines, wav writer, vendored POKEY).
- `docs/audio-provenance.md` — **new**, the verdict table.
- `tests/extract-audio.test.mjs` — **new**.
- `justfile` — **new** `extract-audio` / `extract-audio-all` recipes.

Nothing under a game subrepo is modified. The existing `tools/pokey-bake` and
`tools/speech-bake` directories stay put for now — they are the **comparison
targets**. Retiring them is a follow-on, once the verdict table is green enough to
justify it.

## Verification

1. **Oracle:** tempest's nine ★ sounds render byte-identical to the existing
   `pokey-bake/out/` PCM. Non-negotiable; everything else depends on it.
2. **Speech:** all 23 `SPKVTB` ranges parse out of `SWVOC{2,3}.MAC`, decode to
   intelligible 8 kHz speech through `tms5220.mjs`, and the `remember` /
   `the force will be with you` ordering question is answered with bytes.
3. **Music — dual-encoding cross-check:** every tune in `TUNTAB` parses from *both*
   `SWMUS.SND` (symbolic) and `SWMUS.MAC` (bytes), and the two decode to the
   identical opcode stream. A disagreement is a parser bug or a source
   inconsistency, and either way the tune is `UNVERIFIED` until resolved.
4. **Music — ear check:** the Star Wars theme (`SF2*`) renders to a `.wav` that is
   recognisably the Star Wars theme. This is the one place a human ear is the
   instrument: a VM with a subtly wrong tempo or transposition still produces
   confident, well-formed, *wrong* music, and no byte check will catch it.
5. **Battlezone:** `BZSOUN.MAC` is read for the first time and the standing claim
   in `battlezone/src/shell/audio.ts` ("no ROM register data exists to bake") is
   confirmed or — as expected — falsified.
6. **Honesty:** asteroids reports `NO ROM AUDIO` for every sound, and the run does
   not pretend otherwise.
7. **No-fallback:** deleting `tempest/tools/pokey-bake/sfx-data.mjs` changes no
   verdict. If it does, a fallback leaked in.

## Deferred

- **Promoting verified renders to R2.** There is no bake→R2 path today (it is
  manual). Adding one is only worth doing once the verdict table says what
  deserves to be promoted.
- **Fleet audio convergence.** If battlezone's and red-baron's sounds turn out to
  be ROM-derivable, their bespoke runtime synths become defensible-only for the
  *continuous, parameterised* sounds (engine hum, distance-varying whine) that a
  fixed sample cannot express. The rest could converge on ROM-verified samples
  through `@arcade/shared/audio`. That is a real architectural decision and it
  should be made **after** the audit, on evidence.
