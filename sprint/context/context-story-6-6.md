## Problem
Audio production + integration. Tempest has NO PCM samples in ROM — every sound is live POKEY synthesis. A bake utility ALREADY EXISTS at tools/pokey-bake/ (vendored web-pokey POKEY core, MIT; headless Node; verified producing valid 16-bit 48kHz mono WAV from timed POKEY register sequences in sfx-data.mjs). REMAINING WORK (this story): (1) extract the numeric POKEY envelope data for the 13 catalogued SFX from the rev-3 source and populate tools/pokey-bake/sfx-data.mjs in its register-event format; (2) bake to WAV; (3) host on R2 (arcade-assets.slabgorb.com/tempest/sfx/) and wire/audit vs the current SFX set; prioritize the enemy-fire bolt sound (pairs with 6-5). KEY ROM FACTS (sound-recon over tempest.a65): SFX are envelope programs (target/duration/ramp triples in table Lcbd1) streamed to AUDF/AUDC by update_sounds at the ~246 Hz sound IRQ (NOT 60 Hz) — envelope step N maps to time N/246 s. 13 distinct SFX (table Lcb01 at 0x16867; routines 17166-17435): segment-tick, player-fire, enemy-fire, spike-shot, enemy-death, player-explosion, pulsar-hum, pulsar-active, zoom-start, zoom-through/level-clear, countdown-beep, extra-life, slam. Enemy-fire = sound_enemy_fire at 17179 -> pokey2 voice4, envelopes sample_3b/sample_3e, triggered at 9575 (after sta enm_shotpos,y); shared by ALL enemy types. No dedicated superzapper sound (cascade of enemy-death SFX); no coin sound (mechanical); button-click is a direct-POKEY tone outside the table engine. Full catalog in docs/ux/2026-06-27-tempest-arcade-feel-reference.md (Sound section).
