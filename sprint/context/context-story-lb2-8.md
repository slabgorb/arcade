## Problem
The centrepiece of the 'Arcade Lobby' design, split out of lb2-7 because it is BLOCKED ON DATA, not on markup. The design shows a bordered HIGH SCORES panel that cycles one game at a time on a ~4.5s timer: the game's name in its own glow colour, a TOP FIVE ladder of rank/name/score rows, and a row of pips (one per game, the active one lit in that game's colour). Building the panel is an afternoon. Feeding it is the problem: ADR-0004 decided the lobby reads scores across origins from a cookie carrying a SINGLE BARE NUMBER (arcade-hi-<gameId>=<top score>), so even once lb2-2 ships, the cabinet cannot deliver five rows or a single player NAME to the lobby — and the design needs both. This story must therefore FIRST amend ADR-0004 to widen the published summary from one number to a small top-N row list (name + score; note the 4096 B cookie cap is not the binding constraint — five 3-char names and five scores is well under 200 B), then implement the board on top of it. Depends on lb2-2 (the cookie transport must exist before it can be widened) and composes with lb2-3 (the board must re-read on return from a game, same pageshow entry point). Keep the house fail-soft rule: no readable rows must degrade to an explicit, honest empty state — never to invented names or a zero.

## Acceptance Criteria
- ADR-0004 is amended (or superseded) in writing before the board is built: the published cross-origin summary carries a top-N list of name+score rows rather than a single number, and the amendment states the new cookie shape, its size against the 4096 B cap, and why widening beats the alternatives.
- The board shows a real top-five ladder for the game currently on screen, sourced through the widened @arcade/shared/highscore read — verified per game, not just for one.
- The board rotates through every game in the registry on a timer, and the pip row shows one pip per game with the active pip lit in that game's own colour.
- A game with no readable scores shows an explicit empty state (e.g. NO SCORES YET), never a fabricated name, a placeholder ladder, or a zero.
- The board re-reads on return from a game through lb2-3's single refresh entry point — beating your best and coming back updates the ladder without a manual reload.
- The rotation timer is cleaned up on teardown and does not keep firing against a detached DOM.
