## Problem

`drawWarp` (src/shell/render.ts, ~line 941) currently draws warp-dive speed streaks along `staticTube = s.tube` — the STATIC base well. But during the dive the well expands and the rim flies off; the render loop already computes that moving-eye view at render.ts ~1063-1072, where it selects `warpDiveTube(s.tube, warpProgress)` (during the post-descent eye FLY-IN, `s.warp.flyIn > 0`) or `warpDescentTube(s.tube, warpProgress)` (during descent).

The streaks should ride THAT diving tube's spokes, not the static one. The CLAW, however, must STAY rim-anchored on the static tube — that is tp1-38's deliberate "camera-with-the-Claw" invariant; do NOT regress it.

## Technical Approach

1. Update `drawWarp` to use the diving tube geometry during the descent and fly-in phases
2. Ensure the Claw transformation remains anchored to the static tube
3. Add/extend tests for the streak endpoint geometry if feasible
4. Re-anchor citations after edits to render.ts

## Acceptance Criteria

1. `drawWarp`'s speed streaks are drawn along the DIVING tube's spokes — `warpDescentTube(s.tube, progress)` during descent, and `warpDiveTube(s.tube, warpProgress)` during the eye fly-in (`s.warp.flyIn > 0`), matching how render.ts:1063-1072 already selects the moving-eye well to draw the tube — NOT the static `s.tube`.
