## Problem
Cluster C2, first half. Subsumes W-005, W-006, W-007, W-008, W-009. Enemies in the ROM are a bytecode VM, not state machines. Our five per-kind steppers cannot express, AT ANY CONSTANT SETTING: 'move N frames then flip' (MOVJMP), 'flip continuously while climbing' (SPIRAL), 'reverse direction every 2 jumps then every 3' (SPIRCH), 'flip only when not standing on a spike' (COWJMP), 'flip AWAY from the player' (AVOIDR), or 'crouch 4 frames at the rim then jump toward the player at double angular speed' (TOPPER). This is a rewrite of src/core/enemies/, not a constant tweak. The behavioural tell that the whole cluster is right: a level-1 flipper NEVER flips while climbing.

## Acceptance Criteria
- A CAM interpreter exists in core with the ROM's 20 opcodes, each cited to the source, and is pure/deterministic (no Date, no Math.random — the hard architectural boundary in CLAUDE.md holds).
- All 11 CAM programs are transcribed from the ROM, not paraphrased: MOVJMP, SPIRAL, SPIRCH, COWJMP, AVOIDR, TOPPER and the rest.
- The flipper's program is selected PER WAVE from CAMWAV — not a single hard-coded behaviour.
- ORACLE TEST: on wave 1, a flipper never flips while climbing. This is the single sharpest behavioural difference from our current build and it must be pinned as a test.
- The old per-kind steppers in src/core/enemies/ are deleted, not left dead alongside the interpreter.
- Determinism holds: stepGame(state, input, dt) still produces identical output for identical input, driven by the seeded RNG in GameState.
- npm test -- citations stays green.
