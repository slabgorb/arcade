## Problem
The blimp core (`src/core/blimp.ts`) was built and unit-tested in isolation under
rb2-10, but it is not yet wired into the running game. This story integrates it
into `main.ts` so a blimp actually spawns, drifts, fires, collides, scores, and
despawns during play — through the existing guns/scoring/explosion seams.

## Technical Approach
_Behavior is spec'd by the story title + the rb2 epic guardrails (see
`context-epic-rb2.md`, restored). TEA formalizes the failing tests, Dev makes
them pass. Key integration seams and constraints:_
- Cadence lives at the **calc-frame** rate (~10.42 Hz), not display refresh —
  drift-step per calc-frame; fire on the **÷2** sub-cadence. (epic guardrail #1)
- Render authentic `BLIMP_PICTURE` geometry BROADSIDE — the ROM geometry is
  authored nose-on along local z, so a **yaw** is added to present the broadside.
- The `Enemy` vs `Blimp` `kind` plumbing must be resolved — either widen the
  union or adapt at the `main.ts` boundary.
- Enemy-shell → player-damage must be a **real** path, not a stub.
- Score is a **flat 200** per blimp (epic guardrail #6).

## Scope
- In scope: spawning, drift, render (broadside yaw), firing, player-damage,
  collision/score/explosion wiring, and off-screen despawn — all through
  existing shared seams.
- Out of scope: changing blimp core sim behavior (rb2-10, done); enemy-plane AI;
  ground-wave mechanics.
