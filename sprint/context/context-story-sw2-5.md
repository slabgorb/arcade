## Title
Wire remaining voice lines — only 'Use the Force, Luke' currently fires

## Problem
Voice lines are silent except for "Use the Force, Luke." The Star Wars cabinet
has 23 distinct TMS5220 speech lines, but only one is currently wired to fire at
game events. This blocks the full audiovisual feedback during gameplay — voice
cues are part of the authentic cabinet experience and are essential for player
immersion during combat, terrain phases, and game-over conditions.

## Technical Approach
1. **Inventory the available speech lines** – Review `tools/speech-bake/` output
   and the authentic disassembly (reference/Speech*.asm) to identify all 23 voice
   lines, their names, and their canonical trigger points in the original game.
2. **Expand the `SPEECH` enum** in `src/shell/audio.ts` to include all scripted
   lines (name + R2 filename mapping).
3. **Wire trigger points in the core** – Emit `GameEvent` entries for each voice
   cue point (e.g., round start, enemy spawn, terrain warning, game-over states).
4. **Bind events to `speak()` calls** in the shell's game-loop integration
   (`src/shell/` main/loop).
5. **Test on all phases** – TIE combat, surface assault, trench run, all outcomes.

## Scope
- In scope: all 23 authentic TMS5220 lines from the cabinet, wired to fire at
  their correct in-game trigger points (not just "Use the Force, Luke").
- Out of scope: new voice lines, custom recordings, sound design beyond the
  cabinet's authentic set.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. All 23 TMS5220 voice lines are listed in the `SPEECH` enum (with R2 filenames).
2. Each voice cue is emitted as a `GameEvent` at the correct moment in gameplay
   (e.g., approach trench, round start, enemy destroyed).
3. The shell wires all events to the audio engine's `speak()` method.
4. Playtest: all voice lines fire at expected moments across TIE, surface, and
   trench phases; no voice lines fire out of sequence or at wrong trigger points.
