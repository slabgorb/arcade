THE HEADLINE is the TIMEBASE, not the radix that broke red-baron: movement.ts and firing.ts hard-code FRAMES_PER_SEC=60, but the cabinet game frame is 15.625 Hz (64 ms; NMI 250 Hz divided by 16, 'END OF FRAME (64 MS)' at BZONE.MAC:1085, gated by LSR SYNC at :422). The effect is NOT a uniform 3.84x — the refutation proved it: the tank TURNS 3.84x too fast (pure per-frame quantum), DRIVES forward 1.96x too fast (FORWARD_STEP=48 should be 0x5E=94, the two errors partly cancel), and FIRES shells ~4% slow (the shell integrator already sub-steps 4x per frame). So bz3-1 is a careful per-quantum rebase, NOT a one-line constant change, and it BLOCKS every other numeric story. Note what is NOT wrong: the radix was read correctly everywhere (scores are BCD with implicit x1000), and all 10 of the clone's 3D models are BYTE-EXACT ROM decodes (world-3d filed 0 divergences). Tally: 94 CONFIRMED / 33 DIVERGENCE / 19 NO_COUNTERPART / 13 STRUCTURAL / 2 BOOK_WAS_WRONG. 36 fix-findings clustered into these 13 stories (~41 pts vs 78 raw).
