## Problem
Make high-score NAME entry consistent and actually usable across the cabinet's four scoring games. Today it is neither. tempest cycles letters with the MOUSEWHEEL (also L/R arrows), click/SPACE confirms — nearly unusable, so players just leave the default. asteroids lets you TYPE on the keyboard (the good pattern) but neither game supports BACKSPACE. star-wars and battlezone do not prompt at all — they silently auto-tag a constant ('ACE' and 'AAA' respectively; battlezone's was descoped in bz1-10). Deliberate guiding call (from the user): IGNORE the arcade ROMs' spinner/joystick entry schemes here — they are poor with a keyboard and we have a keyboard. Converge every scoring game on ONE keyboard entry mechanism, with asteroids' direct-typing flow as the reference: type A-Z (uppercased) to fill the initials, Backspace deletes the last character, and the game's existing confirm (start/fire edge) commits. star-wars and battlezone GAIN a real entry screen (replacing the auto-tag), tempest's mousewheel letter-cycle is retired for typing, and asteroids gains Backspace. Share the VERB (the identical keyboard entry mechanism, likely a small PURE helper in @arcade/shared so it stays DOM-free under the purity guard), keep the NUMBERS per-cabinet (max length, styling, confirm key). NO storage change: entries continue through the existing @arcade/shared/highscore seam (HighScoreEntryBase.name — the 3-char initials convention — via insertHighScore + makeHighScoreStorage). red-baron is OUT OF SCOPE: it is a Wave-0 skeleton with no scoring or high-score stack at all (its high-score entry belongs to epic-rb5, GAMODE 7). This story IS the battlezone initials-entry follow-up that bz1-10 left as a non-blocking 'candidate follow-up' Delivery Finding and never materialized — it supersedes that note. Large: a strong candidate to split per-game during planning (pf sprint story split).

## Acceptance Criteria
- All four scoring games (tempest, asteroids, star-wars, battlezone) present a high-score name-entry screen when a score qualifies, and the player enters initials by TYPING on the keyboard (A-Z, uppercased); asteroids' typing flow is the reference and is preserved. tempest's mousewheel/arrow letter-cycle entry is removed; star-wars ('ACE') and battlezone ('AAA') no longer auto-tag a constant.
- Backspace deletes the last entered character in every game's entry flow (including asteroids, which lacks it today). Each game has a test asserting Backspace removes a character and cannot delete past an empty buffer.
- The entry mechanism is consistent across the cabinet: the same keys type a character, Backspace deletes, and each game's existing confirm (start/fire edge) commits once initials are complete. Per-cabinet NUMBERS (max name length, allowed charset, on-screen styling, confirm key) may stay game-specific. The shared mechanism lives in one place (extracted to @arcade/shared, kept pure so the purity guard passes) unless the design documents why local alignment is preferable.
- Entered names persist UNCHANGED through the existing @arcade/shared/highscore seam (HighScoreEntryBase.name, insertHighScore, makeHighScoreStorage): no storage schema, key, or row-guard changes and the 3-char initials convention holds. Commit is edge-triggered so a held key or button does not repeat-confirm and auto-fill the initials (the tempest 6-2 / battlezone held-fire 'AAA' regression); each game has a test that holding confirm across the entry-screen transition does not auto-advance.
- Each affected repo's vitest + vite build are green; a manual run of each of the four games confirms you can type initials, Backspace corrects a mistake, the confirm commits, and the score saves and appears on that game's board and in the lobby high-score tile.
