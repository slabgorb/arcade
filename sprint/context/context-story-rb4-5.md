## Problem
Cluster C4. Subsumes FL-013, FL-014, FL-015, FL-001, FL-004, FL-005. A genuine rewrite, not a constant tweak — say so honestly. The ROM has NO YAW ROTATION: turning adds the scaled PLDELX to UNIV4X, a linear universe-X, and objects are drawn at (their X - UNIV4X) (RBARON.MAC:3200-3213, :2910). It has NO PITCH ROTATION either: climb/dive subtracts the eye height I4YPOS from every object's Y before the single Z rotation and the divide-by-depth (RBGRND.MAC:277-283). We rotate the camera about Y and emit a transient pitch. Also FL-001: the `.4WORD` macro (RBARON.MAC:15-18) MULTIPLIES EVERY OPERAND BY 4, so POTDLY — the pitch table — is 4x what we transcribed. That is an undecoded macro, the classic way this method's findings go wrong, and here it went wrong in OUR code.

## Acceptance Criteria
- The view pipeline is translation-based: world pan via a UNIV4X-equivalent, eye height via an I4YPOS-equivalent, then the ROM's single Z rotation and divide-by-depth. No yaw rotation matrix and no camera pitch remain.
- PITCH_TABLE is re-derived through the `.4WORD` x4 expansion: [-128,-92,-68,-40,-20,0,16,32,52,72,100], not the raw operand list.
- The DISCHK proximity bands are the right way round (the refuter confirmed ours are INVERTED: CLOSE is x0.375 and FAR is x1.0, on a x2 baseline), and ground mode forces the MIDDLE band (0x40 = D6 = x0.625), not the slowest.
- The POT.X turn step is proportional to the error (an arithmetic >>3, bounded [-16,+15]), not a constant ramp; the invented MAX_TURN=40 cap is gone.
- HORIZN = 0x40 = 64 is added to the projected Y of EVERY object (POSITH), and the horizon sits at the finite depth HORZ = 0x1000 = 4096 and moves with altitude.
- This story depends on rb4-1 (its constants are hex) — do not land it first.
