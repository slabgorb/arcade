## Title
Radar scanner — green overlay arc, enemy blips within cone, sweep

## Problem
Battlezone's HUD includes a green **radar scanner** at the top of the viewport:
a circular/arc scope with a rotating **sweep** line, showing **blips** for enemy
craft at their bearing relative to the player's heading. Per the ROM, the 21
obstacles and the saucer are **radar-invisible** (`src/core/obstacles.ts:7`
already records "do NOT appear on radar"; `bz1-9` confirms the saucer exclusion).
No radar exists yet — this story establishes it.

## Technical Approach
_Approach hints for TEA/Dev — the ACs below are the contract; TEA owns the RED
tests._

- **New core module `src/core/radar.ts`** — a pure, deterministic `deriveRadar`
  that maps player pose + a blip-source of world entities into radar blips.
  Downstream stories already assume this name and shape: `bz1-9` wires the live
  saucer through `deriveRadar`'s existing exclusion; `bz1-7` supplies real enemy
  tanks. At bz1-6 there are **no live enemies yet** (bz1-7), so tests drive
  `deriveRadar` against synthetic/stub entities.
- **Bearing math** reuses the established heading convention (`src/core/camera.ts`:
  heading 0 → +Z, π/2 → +X, counter-clockwise; `forwardFromHeading`). A blip's
  angular position is its azimuth **relative to player heading**.
- **Sweep** is a pure function of a monotonic clock/tick → sweep angle, advancing
  at a fixed rate and wrapping over a full revolution. No wall-clock/`Date.now()`
  inside core — pass time in.
- **Shell render** (`src/shell`): the green overlay arc/cone + sweep line + blips,
  drawn at the top of the viewport. Exact placement/bichromatic styling is
  coordinated with `bz1-12` (HUD/cracked-glass framing); bz1-6 establishes the
  scope and its contents.

## Scope
- **In scope:** core `radar.ts` (`deriveRadar` + sweep math) with obstacle- and
  saucer-exclusion built in; shell rendering of the green scanner overlay (arc,
  sweep line, blips) on the viewport.
- **Out of scope:** live enemy-tank entities and AI (`bz1-7`); live saucer wiring
  (`bz1-9` — bz1-6 only builds the exclusion path); final HUD/cracked-glass
  framing and bichromatic palette tuning (`bz1-12`); audio/SFX (`bz1-11`).

## Acceptance Criteria
1. **Blip bearing** — for an entity at a known world position relative to the
   player, `deriveRadar` produces a blip whose bearing equals the entity's
   azimuth relative to the player's `heading` (verified across at least the four
   cardinal relative bearings and a non-cardinal case), following the
   `camera.ts` heading convention.
2. **Obstacle exclusion** — obstacles never produce blips: driving the 21-entry
   obstacle field through `deriveRadar` yields zero obstacle blips.
3. **Saucer exclusion (path only)** — the exclusion path exists so `bz1-9` can
   wire the live saucer with no change to `deriveRadar`'s filter: a
   saucer-typed/flagged entity produces no blip.
4. **Sweep advance** — the sweep angle is a deterministic function of elapsed
   time, advances monotonically at a fixed rate, and wraps cleanly over a full
   revolution (no `Date.now()` inside core).
5. **Render** — the shell draws the green scanner overlay at the top of the
   viewport: the arc/cone outline, the rotating sweep line at the current sweep
   angle, and a mark for each derived blip within the scope. (Visual/manual
   verification acceptable; exact placement deferred to `bz1-12`.)

_TEA may refine/split these during RED; keep the exclusion rules and the
`deriveRadar` name/shape stable — downstream stories depend on them._
