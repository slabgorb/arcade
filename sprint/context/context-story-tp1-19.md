## Scope

**In scope — exactly three live findings** (these match the four ACs):

1. **V-017 — the TEMPEST logo's own alphabet (TEMLIT).** ALVROM.MAC 1297-1351
   (CM=1 CD=1 CB=6). SEVEN dedicated logo-letter routines with their own geometry —
   e.g. a BACK-SLANTED E whose arms step left as they descend. The layout is NOT a
   straight baseline: after CNTR it moves (-1B0,100), draws T, +60 E, +24 M, +34 P,
   then JUMPS (0F8,48) up-and-right, draws E, +16,+28 S, then +60,-60 T — so "TEMP"
   sits low-left and "EST" stair-steps up and right. Ours renders the string
   "TEMPEST" through the ordinary message font (`render.ts` ~679 → `vecText` →
   `layoutText`) on one horizontal baseline. Verified this is genuinely its own
   alphabet, not the ANVGAN message font (CHAR.E/CHAR.T are structurally unrelated).
   → AC-1.

2. **V-015 — the star pictures (MSTAR1-MSTAR4).** ALVROM.MAC 405-515 (CM=4 CD=1):
   four macros of SCDOTs at authored absolute coordinates. **MSTAR1 has 22 dots;
   MSTAR2/3/4 have 20 each** — AC-2's "the ROM's 22 dots" names MSTAR1 specifically,
   so do not force all four to 22. Ours reuses four 5-dot pictures of hand-picked
   unit vectors scattered radially from screen centre (`render.ts` ~133-138) — a
   quarter of the arcade's density. Colour is right for waves 1-4 (BLUE,
   ALDISP.MAC 2952) but from wave 5 the ROM varies star colour per plane
   (ALDISP.MAC 2949-2960), which we never do. → AC-2.

3. **V-022 — FUSEX1/2/3 are floating score numbers, not explosions.**
   ALVROM.MAC 1096-1114: each sets CSTAT WHITE, SCAL 1,20, backs up `VCTR -36.,0,0`,
   then JSRLs digit glyphs — FUSEX1='750', FUSEX2='500', FUSEX3='250'. They bloom
   where a fuseball dies (`PITAB FUSEX1,PTFUSX`, ALVROM.MAC 2148). We render no
   floating score text anywhere.
   ⚠️ **V-022 is the only one of the three with no [REFUTATION] re-verification pass
   in its reasoning — TEA must verify the FUSEX line range and digit values against
   ALVROM.MAC directly during RED.**
